Experimental setup

The Random Positioning Machine is a device that can simulate microgravity and partial gravity, designed by Airbus Defence and Space. By constantly rotating, it averages out effective gravity on longer time scales, as for bacterial growth are involved. In this way, we can study the effects of lower gravitational levels on bacteria in our labs on earth.
Four strains were studied this experiment:

  • E. coli DH5α
  • E. coli TOP10
  • E. coli BW25113
  • Pseudomonas putida S12
First the time needed to get OD of around 0.4 is determined for each strain. Next different dilution are tested to obtain the desired number of colonies on each plate. Finally this knowledge is used to grow around 50 colonies on a plate both in and next to the RPM to monitor differences in morphology.

Inoculating strains from plates

Day1: 2016/07/11
Investigator(s): Valentijn Broeken
Procedure: After the strains were taken from the freezer and grown on solid LB medium, colonies were transferred to liquid LB medium. By making a dilution series of this culture, the optimal bacterial density (i.e. preferred dilution series) to be used on the plates in the RPM will be determined.

  • For each strain, a 100 mL erlenmeyer was filled with 20 mL LB medium.
  • Seven colonies per strain were transferred from the plates to the flask using an öse.
  • These were grown overnight at 30 °C and 200 rpm, starting at 17:00.

Incubating the strains in the RPM for phenotype study in simulated microgravity

Day4: 2016/07/20
Investigator(s): Valentijn Broeken, Vincent de Bakker
Procedure: At 20:30 the OD values of the cultures were measured. By accident, the OD was measured at 546nm, while the OD had to be measured at 600nm. The OD-values were:

Strain OD546nm
TOP10 0.371
BW25113 0.420
Pseudomonas 0.263
DH5α 0.263

The Pseudomonas was placed back in the incubater for another 45 minutes during the dilution steps of the other strains. After that, the OD was measured at 600nm and was found to be 0.750.

The anticipated OD600 at this time would be 0.4. The bacterial cultures were diluted 10-6.5 (100x > 100x > 50x) and 10-7 times (another 2x). Dilution steps were done in duplo and each of the two final dilutions per duplo were plated twice, one for 1g and one for 0g. There are thus four 10-6.5 dilutions and four 10-7 dilutions plated for each strain. Since the OD600 was far too high for Pseudomonas, this culture was diluted 2x before the standard dilutions described above.

At 22:45h the plates were put at 30°C besides (1g) or in the RPM (0g). It is however thought that the incubator's temperature is too low, because the next morning no colonies could be seen.
